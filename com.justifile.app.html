JustiFile Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 3, 2026 · Last updated: May 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information JustiFile ("we", "us", "the App") collects, how it's used, and your rights. JustiFile is published by Edward Goldenberg. Contact: edwardgoldenberg@gmail.com.

Information We Collect

Identifiers used for advertising

JustiFile uses Google AdMob to display ads. To deliver personalized ads, AdMob may collect your device's advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS, AAID on Android), a coarse approximation of your location derived from your IP address, and ad-interaction data (which ads you've seen and tapped). On iOS, the App will request your permission via the system "App Tracking Transparency" prompt before this identifier is used to track you across apps; if you decline, only non-personalized contextual ads are shown. The Google Mobile Ads SDK also collects device performance data (app launch time, hang rate, energy usage) and crash logs to ensure ads render correctly. See Google's Privacy Policy and AdMob's data disclosure document for full details on AdMob's data practices.

Purchases

In-app purchases (JustiFile Pro and Pro+ subscriptions) are processed by Apple App Store / Google Play. We do not see or store your payment details. Receipts are validated through the platform's StoreKit / Play Billing APIs. Subscription entitlement state is managed through RevenueCat, which receives an anonymized purchase identifier; no personal information is sent.

Camera and Photos

The App requests access to your camera and photo library only when you tap a feature that needs it (attaching evidence such as receipts or screenshots to your complaint). The captured media is stored locally on your device and is included in the complaint PDF only if you choose to attach it. Media is not uploaded to any server we control.

Complaint content (on-device)

The complaint information you enter (company name, incident description, your name and email, attached evidence) is stored locally on your device using SQLite. When you tap "Submit," the App opens your default mail client with a pre-filled message addressed to the applicable Attorney General office(s); you remain in control of sending. JustiFile does not transmit your complaint content to any backend we operate.

How We Use Information

  • To provide and maintain the App's functionality.
  • To display advertisements via Google AdMob (the only third-party advertising provider used).
  • To process subscription purchases via Apple, Google, and RevenueCat.

Data Sharing

We do not sell or rent your personal information to anyone. We share data only with the third-party services listed above (each linked to its own privacy policy):

  • Google AdMob — advertising identifier for ad targeting and measurement.
  • Apple / Google Play — subscription billing.
  • RevenueCat — subscription entitlement management (anonymized purchase identifier only).

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction (GDPR in Europe, CCPA in California, similar laws elsewhere) you may have the right to:

  • Access the data we hold about you.
  • Request deletion of your data. The App provides "Export all my data" and "Delete all my data" controls in Settings.
  • Opt out of tracking or personalized advertising (use iOS "Limit Ad Tracking" or Android "Reset advertising ID" / "Opt out of Ads Personalization").

To exercise any of these rights, email us at edwardgoldenberg@gmail.com.

Children's Privacy

JustiFile is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

Data Retention

Complaint data and attached evidence are retained on your device until you delete them via the in-app "Delete all my data" control or uninstall the App. Subscription entitlement data is retained by RevenueCat for as long as your subscription is active; advertising identifier data retention is governed by Google's policies.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
